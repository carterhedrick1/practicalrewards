Practical Recommendation

The Gold Card makes sense if you spend real money on food—think groceries at U.S. supermarkets and eating out—and you don't mind juggling a few monthly dining credits. The annual fee is on the higher side, so you want to actually use the credits and the strong earning rates (4 points per dollar at restaurants and at U.S. supermarkets up to a yearly cap, 3 points per dollar on flights) to come out ahead.

If you hate tracking little monthly perks, skip this. But if groceries and dining are a big slice of your budget, this card can quietly stack a lot of value, no spreadsheets required. Bottom line: food-first households who will actually use the credits will be happy; everyone else may prefer a simple flat-cash-back card.

Practical Advice

This is what makes or breaks a card for you. If you can use these credits in your normal spend, the perks and protections are all bonus for you. We have a calculator at the bottom of this section to help you calculate that. Below we break down exactly what every credit is to show you if it's easy or if they're going to make you jump through 17 hoops to redeem it.

  • $120 Uber Cash – Up to $10 in Uber Cash each month for rides or Uber Eats orders in the United States. Add the card to your Uber account, keep it selected as the payment method, and you'll see $10 in Uber Cash load monthly. Enrollment required. Credits don't roll over. Since late 2024, an American Express card must be selected at checkout to redeem the Uber Cash. Uber Cash is app-wallet money, not a statement credit; you must keep the card active in the Uber wallet and selected at checkout. One Uber account gets the benefit per card. Great if you already use Uber or Uber Eats monthly; worth $0 if you never do.
  • $120 Dining Credit – Up to $10 per month in statement credits at select merchants: Grubhub, The Cheesecake Factory, Goldbelly, Wine.com, and Five Guys. Enrollment required. Enroll, pay with the card at an eligible merchant, and up to $10 posts back as a statement credit each calendar month. No rollover—use it or lose it each month. Eligible merchants are limited; check the list. Easy money if you already order from these brands; not worth forcing just to "use the credit."
  • $100 Resy Credit – Up to $100 per year for purchases at restaurants on Resy in the United States, delivered in two chunks (up to $50 January–June and up to $50 July–December). Enrollment required. Enroll, pay with the card at a U.S. Resy restaurant or for an eligible Resy purchase, and the credit posts. Semi-annual windows; if you miss one half of the year, that $50 is gone. U.S. Resy restaurants only. Great if you dine out a couple of times a year at Resy spots; skip the mental gymnastics if you don't. Check out restaurants on Resy to see if you already go to one of these.
  • $84 Dunkin' Credit – Up to $7 per month in statement credits at U.S. Dunkin' locations. Enrollment required. Enroll, pay with the card at Dunkin', get up to $7 back each month. Monthly use-it-or-lose-it; only at Dunkin' in the United States. Perfect for regular Dunkin' people; everyone else will forget and forfeit it.

Perks

  • Strong earning on food and flights – Earn 4 points per dollar at restaurants worldwide, 4 points per dollar at U.S. supermarkets (on up to $25,000 per year, then 1 point), and 3 points per dollar on flights booked directly with airlines or through American Express Travel; everything else earns 1 point per dollar. If you spend heavily on food, this is where the card pays.
  • The Hotel Collection – When you book a prepaid, two-night minimum stay through American Express Travel, you can get a property credit (often $100 toward dining, spa, or similar) and a room upgrade when available. Good for occasional splurge weekends; not a rebate, but the perks can be nice.
  • No foreign transaction fees – Take the card abroad without an extra fee tacked onto each purchase. Simple, useful, and the way it should be.
  • Amex Offers – Targeted rebates and extra point promos that can stack with normal earning; worth checking before big purchases. (Think of it as surprise coupons in your account.)

Protections

  • Trip Delay Insurance – Reimburses reasonable expenses like meals and a hotel if your round-trip flight is delayed a long time (coverage and trigger time are defined in the benefits; Gold's plan shows up to $300 per trip with longer delays required than premium cards). Helpful when weather or operational messes strand you.
  • Baggage Insurance Plan – Coverage for lost, damaged, or stolen baggage when you pay the entire fare with the card; typical limits on Gold show up to $1,250 for carry-on and up to $500 for checked baggage, in excess of the carrier's coverage. Handy if your bag vanishes or arrives broken.
  • Car Rental Loss and Damage Insurance – Secondary coverage for theft or damage to the rental car when you pay with the card and decline the rental company's collision damage waiver. Good for standard rentals; not a substitute for liability coverage.
  • Purchase Protection – Covers eligible new purchases against accidental damage or theft for a limited time after purchase, with per-claim and yearly caps (Gold's current terms show up to $10,000 per covered purchase, up to $50,000 per account per calendar year). Great for gadgets and gifts that might break right after the return window.
  • Extended Warranty – Adds one extra year to the manufacturer's warranty for eligible items with original warranties of five years or less (benefit limits apply). Can save you from buying extra coverage at checkout.

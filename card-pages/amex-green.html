Practical Recommendation

If you want a basic travel card that just works, this is it. Green gives 3x on travel, transit, and dining with no portals, caps, or rotating hoops. The $150 annual fee is straightforward, and there aren't monthly nickels-and-dimes to track—there's basically just one optional Clear Plus credit you can use or ignore.

Who it fits: people who actually spend on flights, hotels, and getting around town and want points without babysitting a stack of credits. If you want a coupon book and lounge hype, look elsewhere. Bottom line: a simple, good-value travel card that actually rewards travel.

Protections

(use the card to pay for the trip; terms apply):

  • Trip Delay Insurance – Reimburses up to $300 per trip for essentials (hotel, meals) when a covered round-trip flight is delayed 12+ hours; up to 2 claims per 12 months. Handy for weather meltdowns and missed connections.
  • Baggage Insurance Plan – Coverage for lost, damaged, or stolen baggage on eligible tickets paid with the card (typical limits: up to ~$1,250 carry-on / ~$500 checked; see your guide for exact amounts and exclusions).
  • Car Rental Loss & Damage Insurance (secondary) – Pays for damage/theft to the rental car when you decline the agency's collision damage waiver and pay with the card; liability not included. Optional paid Premium Protection available.
  • Purchase Protection – Covers theft/accidental damage for 90 days, up to $1,000 per item and $50,000 per account per calendar year. Good backstop for gadgets and small appliances.
  • Extended Warranty – Adds up to 1 extra year to eligible manufacturer warranties of 5 years or less on covered purchases. Useful on electronics that come with 1-year coverage.

Practical Recommendation

This is the "I actually stay at Hilton" card. The annual free night, Hilton Honors Diamond status, and the two big statement credits (resort and flights) do the heavy lifting. If you can hit the resort credit twice a year and remember the quarterly flight credit, the high annual fee becomes a rounding error.

If you do not visit Hilton resorts, rarely fly, or hate tracking credits, skip this. Bottom line: frequent Hilton guest with even a couple of trips a year? Easy keeper. Otherwise, you will pay $550 to babysit benefits you will not use.

  • $400 Hilton Resort Credit – Up to $200 Jan–Jun and $200 Jul–Dec back on eligible purchases at participating Hilton resorts when paid with the card. Works on room charges and many on-property purchases that bill to your room. Simple if you already book resorts; zero value if you never do.
  • $200 Flight Credit – $50 per calendar quarter back on flight purchases made directly with airlines or through Amex Travel. Not incidental fees; it works on airfare. If you buy at least one ticket each quarter, this is easy money.

  • $209 CLEAR Plus Credit – Up to $209 per calendar year back after you pay for CLEAR Plus with the card. Great if your airports have CLEAR; otherwise it is a shrug.

Perks

  • Earning rates (the short version) – 14x at Hilton hotels & resorts booked direct; 7x flights booked direct or via amex travel; 7x U.S. restaurants and eligible car rentals; 3x everything else. Simple: use it for Hilton, flights, and dining. Limits: "U.S." categories apply only in the United States; see terms for what counts as direct.
  • Hilton Honors Diamond Status – Room upgrades (when available), breakfast credit at many full-service U.S. brands, lounge access where lounges exist, better points earnings, and premium Wi-Fi. This pays off if you stay at Hilton a few nights per year; if you do not, it is just a shiny badge.
  • Annual Free Night Rewards – One Free Night Reward every renewal; earn an additional Free Night Reward at $30,000 calendar-year spend and another at $60,000. Redeemable at almost all Hilton brands, including many high-end resorts, subject to a few exclusions. Book by phone when the certificate appears in your Hilton account.
  • National Car Rental Emerald Club Executive status – Pick from the Executive area and often pay a mid-size rate. Helpful at busy airports; enrollment required.
  • No foreign transaction fees – Use it abroad without extra currency fees.

Protections

  • Trip Delay Insurance – If a round-trip paid entirely with the card is delayed 6+ hours, get reimbursed for essentials like meals and lodging, up to $500 per trip (limits apply). Great for weather meltdowns and missed connections.
  • Trip Cancellation & Interruption Insurance – For covered reasons on round-trips paid entirely with the card, non-refundable expenses can be covered up to $10,000 per trip and $20,000 per 12 months. Use when illness or severe weather ruins plans.
  • Car Rental Loss & Damage Insurance – Secondary coverage for theft or damage when you pay with the card and decline the rental agency's collision damage waiver. Good for saving at the counter; liability is not included.
  • Baggage Insurance Plan – Coverage for carry-on and checked bags when travel is paid with the card; useful if the airline loses or damages your stuff.
  • Purchase Protection – New purchases can be covered for theft or accidental damage for up to 90 days, typically up to $10,000 per claim (caps apply). Handy for phones, gadgets, and furniture mishaps.
  • Extended Warranty – Adds one year to eligible U.S. manufacturer warranties of 5 years or less, up to $10,000 per item (account caps apply). This can save you from buying store plans.
  • Cell Phone Protection – Pay your monthly wireless bill with the card and get up to $800 per claim, 2 claims per 12 months, $50 deductible. Good backup if you skip AppleCare-style add-ons.

Practical Recommendation

Want one card to swipe everywhere with no mental load? This is that—1.5% back on everything (bumped to 2% in year one). The value jumps if you bank with Bank of America or Merrill and qualify for Preferred Rewards, which boosts your earned cash back by 25%–75%. If you don't have that status, a plain 2% card is usually the higher floor.

Bottom line: keep if you're in the BofA ecosystem or want a simple starter; skip if you chase best-in-class cash back.

Perks

  • Earning structure (set-and-forget) – 2% on all purchases in the first 12 months, then 1.5% unlimited. Rewards don't expire while the account is open. Good "one card" setup if you don't want categories.
  • Preferred Rewards boost (25%–75%) – If you hold qualifying balances with Bank of America/Merrill and enroll, your earned cash back gets a 25%/50%/75% bonus (e.g., 1.5% → 1.875% / 2.25% / 2.625%). Doesn't change the base categories—just multiplies what you already earned.
  • Flexible redemptions – Redeem as a statement credit or deposit to eligible Bank of America or Merrill accounts; automatic redemptions typically start at $25. Handy if you like set-and-forget payouts.

Protections

  • $0 liability for fraud – You're not on the hook for unauthorized charges when reported promptly.
  • Purchase protection – Helps with theft or accidental damage shortly after purchase; limits and windows vary by your benefits guide.
  • Extended warranty – Adds time to eligible manufacturer warranties (typically up to one additional year on shorter warranties).
  • Auto rental collision damage waiver – Collision/theft coverage when you pay with the card and decline the rental counter's coverage (usually secondary).
  • Travel & emergency assistance – A hotline for referrals (doctors, transport, ticket replacement). You pay the service provider; the benefit is coordination.
  • Heads-up: foreign transaction fee – Charges 3% on purchases outside the U.S., so it's not your international card.

If you bank with Bank of America or Merrill, you can boost your cash back by 25% to 75%. Here's how it works:

How to Qualify

Step 1: Open a Bank of America checking account (required)

Step 2: Maintain qualifying balances across your accounts:

  • What counts: Any mix of Bank of America deposits + Merrill accounts (IRAs and 529s included)
  • How to qualify: Keep the tier's 3-month average, or get early enrollment by hitting the balance within 30 days of opening your checking account

What It Does to This Card

Preferred Rewards boosts your earned cash back by:

  • Gold: +25% boost
  • Platinum: +50% boost
  • Platinum Honors: +75% boost (maximum for credit cards)

The boost applies to your earned cash back after purchase, but doesn't change the base earning rates.

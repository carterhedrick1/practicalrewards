Practical Recommendation

This is the "live in United Clubs" card. If United is your primary airline, the built-in United Club membership, two free checked bags for you and a companion, Premier Access, and a pile of partner credits can make the $695 feel fair. You also get better earn on United spend than the mid-tier cards.

If you don't actually visit lounges or fly United often, the credits will be homework and the fee will sting. Bottom line: frequent United flyers who value lounge time will like it; everyone else should consider a cheaper United card.

Perks

  • Earning Rate – 4x United purchases • 2x travel • 2x dining • 1x everything else
  • United Club Membership – Full membership with entry for you plus 1 adult guest and your dependent children under 18; unlock All Access membership (more locations and up to 2 adult guests) with Premier Gold status or $50,000 calendar-year spend on this card. Useful if a Club sits in your regular airports.
  • Star Alliance Partner Lounges – Use participating Star Alliance-member lounges when flying same-day on Star Alliance. Handy on international trips when a United Club isn't nearby. Guest rules vary by lounge.
  • Two Free Checked Bags – First and second checked bags free for you and one companion on the same reservation; can save $160+ roundtrip for two. Terms apply.
  • Premier Access – Priority check-in, security lane (where available), and boarding; saves time on busy days.
  • Award Discounts – 10% off United Economy Saver awards within the continental U.S. and Canada; plus up to 2 discounted award flights per year after each $20,000 in purchases (max 2 per account year). Good if you book awards often.
  • 25% Back In-Flight & Club Premium Drinks – Rebate on eligible United in-flight purchases and United Club premium drinks. Small but automatic.
  • Hotel & Car Status – IHG One Rewards Platinum Elite and Avis President's Club; can mean upgrades and better cars at the counter.
  • No Foreign Transaction Fees – Use abroad without extra surcharges.
  • MileagePlus Perks – Mileage accelerator options and PQP earnings when you book United-operated flights with your MileagePlus number.
  • JSX Partnership – Access to JSX, a crowd-free hop-on jet service offering private jet-style amenities (no TSA lines, comfortable seating, free snacks/drinks) at commercial airline prices. Serves select routes primarily on the West Coast and Texas.

Protections

  • Trip Cancellation/Interruption – Up to $10,000 per covered traveler and $20,000 per trip (max $40,000 per 12-month period per account) for covered reasons. Helps if plans get blown up.
  • Trip Delay – If delayed 12+ hours or overnight, up to $500 per covered traveler per trip for hotels/meals/essentials.
  • Baggage Delay – After a 6-hour delay, $100/day up to 3 days for essentials.
  • Lost Luggage – Reimbursement for checked or carry-on baggage lost/damaged by a carrier (limits apply).
  • Auto Rental Collision (Primary) – Primary coverage for theft/damage when you decline the rental agency's CDW and pay with the card.
  • Purchase Protection & Extended Warranty – Protection for new purchases (limits/timeframes apply) and 1 extra year on eligible manufacturer warranties of 3 years or less.

United Lounges

A big perk of United Club℠ Infinite (Chase) is lounge access — a built-in United Club membership. If a Club lives at your home airport, it's a real quality-of-life upgrade: decent food, working seats with outlets, showers at hubs, and usually calmer than the gate. United has been opening larger Clubs (Denver) and adding Club Fly grab-and-go concepts (Houston), so check locations and hours in the app before you bank on one.

Guest Access

  • United Club (membership included with this card) — Primary cardholders receive United Club Card membership (digital card in the United app). Admits you + 1 adult guest, plus your dependent children under 18 per visit. A same-day boarding pass is required; access and rules follow United's Club policies and capacity controls. Note: this base membership does not include Star Alliance partner lounges.
  • United Club All Access (earnable upgrade) — Earn All Access after $50,000 in calendar-year card spend or by holding Premier Gold (or higher). All Access adds Star Alliance partner lounge access when flying same-day on a Star Alliance airline and increases guesting to 2 adults (or 1 adult + dependent children under 18). Valid for the rest of that year and the following program year.
  • United Polaris Lounges (not included with membership) — These are a different product with stricter rules; membership from this card does not grant entry. Access is limited to eligible long-haul international business-class customers (and select partners) on the same day.

United Club (U.S. & select international)

  • Denver (DEN) — Concourse B West near B32 (new, very large); B East near B44; plus Club Fly near B60 for grab-and-go. Train links A/B/C airside.
  • Chicago–O'Hare (ORD) — T1 near B6, B18, C10; additional Club in T2/F9; Polaris by C18 (separate rules). Pedestrian tunnel links B↔C airside.
  • Newark (EWR) — Terminal C near C74, C93, C123. Use AirTrain to move between terminals landside.
  • Houston Intercontinental (IAH) — Terminal C (North & South) and Terminal E; Terminal B United Club closed Jan 2025 for Terminal B redevelopment; Club Fly debuted in B. Skylink connects terminals airside.
  • Washington–Dulles (IAD) — Clubs near C7, C17, and D8; Polaris near C17 (separate rules). Mobile lounge/train connects concourses airside.
  • Los Angeles (LAX) — Main Club in Terminal 7 (United concourse); walkway to Terminal B (TBIT) airside.
  • San Francisco (SFO) — T3/E near E4, T3/F rotunda near F11, International G between G6–G9; new T2 Club planned. Terminals 3 and G connect airside.
  • Boston (BOS) — Terminal B near United gates; terminals connect landside; limited airside connectors.
  • Orlando (MCO) — Concourse B near B43.
  • Philadelphia (PHL) — Level 2 between Concourses C–D.
  • Phoenix (PHX) — Terminal 3, near E3.
  • More cities — United lists 45+ Clubs systemwide and updates hours/closures frequently; check the live list before you rely on a specific location.

Heads-up: The membership from this card starts at the Card membership level (you + 1 adult guest + dependent kids; no Star Alliance access). If you want partner lounge access and 2 adult guests, you'll need to earn All Access via $50,000 annual spend or Premier Gold (or higher). Polaris lounges remain off-limits unless your ticket qualifies.

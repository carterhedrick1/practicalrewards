Practical Recommendation

This is an easy keeper if your spending lives in groceries, gas, and online shopping. No annual fee, simple cash back, and the caps are big enough for most households.

If you max the 3% categories, great; after $6,000 per category per year, rotate to a different card. Bottom line: a straightforward starter or forever-no-fee card.

Gotchas: subscription required; credits do not roll; limited to eligible online purchases; availability varies by state. Practical value: great if you already use Home Chef; skip if meal kits aren't your thing. — See Home Chef credit

Perks

  • Earning rates (detailed) – 3% at U.S. supermarkets, 3% at U.S. online retail purchases, 3% at U.S. gas stations (each up to $6,000 per year, then 1%); 1% on everything else. Simple cash back as statement credits.
  • Amex Offers – Targeted rebates at select retailers and services. Why use it: real cash savings on stuff you might buy anyway. Catch: activation required per offer; expiration and caps apply. — See Amex Offers

Protections

  • Purchase Protection – Covers theft or accidental damage for new purchases for up to 90 days. Why it matters: helps when something you just bought gets damaged or stolen. Limits and exclusions apply (per-item and annual caps). — See Purchase Protection terms
  • Car Rental Loss & Damage Insurance (secondary) – Coverage for damage or theft of a rental car when you pay with the card and decline the rental company's collision damage waiver. Why use it: can save you from buying the rental counter's add-on. Catch: secondary coverage; liability not included; vehicle and country restrictions apply. — See Car Rental LDW terms

Practical Recommendation

If you already bank with Bank of America and can qualify for Preferred Rewards, this card is an easy keeper: $0 annual fee, a 3% category you can switch monthly, and a useful 2% tier—great for everyday spend (within the $2,500/quarter cap), not big one-off purchases.

If you don't bank with Bank of America, this card usually isn't the standout. Citi Custom Cash is simpler and typically richer: it automatically gives 5% on your top eligible category each billing cycle (up to $500), no toggling required. Pair that with a basic 2% everywhere card and you'll beat 3% most months.

Perks

  • Earning structure (set-and-forget, with a knob) – 3% in one category you choose (gas & EV charging, online, dining, travel, drug stores, or home improvement/furnishings), 2% at grocery stores and wholesale clubs, 1% on everything else. The 3%/2% rates share a $2,500 quarterly cap; after that, it's 1%. You can change your 3% category once per calendar month; default is gas & EV charging. Helpful if your spending pattern changes seasonally.
  • Preferred Rewards bonus (25%–75%) – If you keep qualifying balances with Bank of America/Merrill, your earned cash back gets a 25%–75% boost when the purchase posts. Great for BofA loyalists; irrelevant if you do not bank there.
  • First-year 6% boost – New cardholders get 6% in the chosen 3% category for the first 12 months from account opening (then it returns to 3%). Good for front-loading value on expenses you can time (e.g., big online purchases or a home project).

Protections

  • Extended warranty – Adds up to one extra year on eligible items with manufacturer warranties of three years or less. Useful for gadgets and appliances; keep receipts and pay with the card.
  • $0 liability for fraud – Standard fraud protection when you report unauthorized charges promptly.
  • Foreign transaction fee (heads-up) – Charges 3% on purchases outside the U.S., so it's a poor pick for international trips.

Preferred Rewards: How to Boost Your Cash Back

If you bank with Bank of America or Merrill, you can boost your cash back by 25% to 75%. Here's how it works:

How to Qualify

Step 1: Open a Bank of America checking account (required)

Step 2: Maintain qualifying balances across your accounts:

  • What counts: Any mix of Bank of America deposits + Merrill accounts (IRAs and 529s included)
  • How to qualify: Keep the tier's 3-month average, or get early enrollment by hitting the balance within 30 days of opening your checking account

What It Does to This Card

Preferred Rewards boosts your earned cash back by:

  • Gold: +25% boost
  • Platinum: +50% boost
  • Platinum Honors: +75% boost (maximum for credit cards)

The boost applies to your earned cash back after purchase, but doesn't change the $2,500 quarterly cap or your ability to change categories monthly.

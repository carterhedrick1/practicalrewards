Practical Recommendation

This card makes sense if you actually use the credits and lounges. The math is straightforward: the $300 airline incidental credit + $150 lifestyle credit + regular travel spend can erase most (or all) of the $550 annual fee.

Frequent flyers or anyone who wants Priority Pass for multiple people will get the most value. If you do not travel much or do not want to track credits, a cheaper travel card (or simple cash back) will be less annoying. Bottom line: fly a few times a year and use the credits, and it can pay for itself.

Perks

  • Earning rates, at a glance – 3x travel via Bank of America Travel; 2x travel & dining; 1.5x everything else. Simple to keep track of if most of your spend is travel/dining.
  • Priority Pass Lounges – Up to four Priority Pass Select memberships per account (primary + up to three more) with complimentary lounge entry for enrolled members; guest policies vary by lounge. Good if you want lounge access for family or travel partners. — See lounge list
  • Airfare discount when paying with points – Save 20% on airfare when you redeem points for flights through Bank of America Travel (only the points-paid portion gets the discount). Helpful if you redeem for flights often. — Air savings program details
  • Preferred Rewards boost – Hold $20,000–$100,000+ with Bank of America/Merrill and earn 25%–75% more points on every purchase. Worth it if you already bank/invest there. — Learn about Preferred Rewards
  • No foreign transaction fees – Use it abroad without the 1%–3% penalty typical on basic cards.
  • Visa Infinite concierge & hotel collection – 24/7 help for reservations plus perks at select hotels (breakfast, late checkout when available). Nice to have, not magic. — See Visa Infinite Luxury Hotel Collection

Protections

  • Trip Delay Insurance – Up to $500 per ticket for meals/lodging after a 6+ hour delay or overnight; covers you, spouse/partner, and dependent kids when you pay the common-carrier fare with the card.
  • Trip Cancellation/Interruption – Up to $2,500 per trip for covered reasons (illness, injury, carrier insolvency, etc.) when travel was paid with the card.
  • Baggage Delay – $100 per day (up to 5 days) for essentials after a 6+ hour baggage delay.
  • Common Carrier Travel Accident – Up to $1,000,000 accidental death and dismemberment when traveling on a paid common-carrier ticket.
  • Rental Car Insurance (Primary CDW) – Primary coverage up to the car's actual cash value (most vehicles up to $75,000 MSRP) for theft/damage when you pay with the card and decline the agency's coverage.
  • Emergency Evacuation & Repatriation – Up to $100,000 for evacuation and $10,000 for repatriation on covered trips when arranged through the benefits administrator.
  • Purchase Protection – Covers new purchases against damage/theft for 90 days; up to $10,000 per claim, $50,000 per cardholder.
  • Return Protection – If a retailer refuses a return within 90 days, up to $300 per item, $1,000 per year.
  • Extended Warranty – Adds up to 1 extra year on eligible U.S. manufacturer warranties of 3 years or less; up to $10,000 per claim, $50,000 per cardholder.

What It Does to This Card

Preferred Rewards boosts your earned points by:

  • Gold: +25% boost
  • Platinum: +50% boost
  • Platinum Honors: +75% boost (maximum for credit cards)

The boost applies to your earned points after purchase, turning 3x travel via BofA Travel into up to 5.25x, 2x travel/dining into up to 3.5x, and 1.5x everything else into up to 2.625x.

Practical Recommendation

Fly Delta a few times a year? This card makes economy trips less annoying: your first checked bag is free, you board earlier, and award tickets price 15% lower. The $100 Delta Stays credit is easy if you ever book a prepaid hotel through Delta's portal.

If you can push $10,000 of spend in a calendar year, the $200 Delta flight credit sweetens things; otherwise, value leans on the bag perk and TakeOff 15. Bottom line: simple, solid for Delta flyers who do not want to chase elite status or lounge perks.

Below we break down exactly what every credit is to show you if it's easy or if they're going to make you jump through 17 hoops to redeem it.

  • $100 Delta Stays Credit – Up to $100 back per calendar year when you use the card to book prepaid hotels or vacation rentals via Delta Stays on delta.com. Credit applies automatically after the charge posts. Prices can differ from booking direct, and taxes/fees do not count toward any minimums. Good if you already make one prepaid booking a year—otherwise worth $0.
  • $200 Delta Flight Credit (after $10,000 calendar-year spend) – Spend $10,000 in purchases in a calendar year and receive a $200 Delta flight credit for future Delta-issued airfare. It applies to the airfare (and taxes/fees on tickets), not extras like bags or onboard purchases. Posts after you cross the threshold; use on delta.com or by phone. Great if you reliably fly Delta; irrelevant if you do not hit $10,000.
  • Uber One Monthly Credit (limited-time) – Get a statement credit for up to $9.99 per month for up to 6 consecutive months when you pay for an Uber One monthly membership with this card by Jun 25, 2026. Auto-renewal applies; stop paying with the card to stop credits. Nice if you already wanted Uber One; skip if not.

Perks

  • Earning structure (quick recap) – 2x on Delta purchases; 2x at restaurants worldwide (including U.S. takeout and delivery); 2x at U.S. supermarkets; 1x on everything else. Straightforward everyday categories that many households hit.
  • TakeOff 15 (award discount) – Save 15% in miles on Delta-operated award tickets when booking on delta.com or the Fly Delta app (taxes/fees still apply). Helps stretch miles for domestic trips and flash sales.
  • First Checked Bag Free – The primary cardmember and up to 8 companions on the same reservation get the first checked bag free on Delta-operated flights when the card is used as specified in the terms. That is up to $70 saved per person on a round-trip.
  • Priority Boarding (Zone 5 / Main Cabin 1) – Board earlier with your travel party so you can snag overhead space without the shuffle. Cardmember plus up to 8 companions on the same reservation.
  • In-flight savings – Get 20% back as a statement credit on eligible Delta onboard purchases (snacks and drinks; Wi-Fi typically excluded). Handy for a cheaper coffee at 30,000 feet.
  • Pay with Miles – Use miles to offset the cash cost of Delta tickets at checkout on delta.com; simple and flexible if you prefer cash-like redemptions over award hunting.

Protections

  • Car Rental Loss and Damage Insurance (secondary) – When you pay in full with the card and decline the rental company's waiver, you are covered for theft or damage to most rentals (secondary to your own insurance); country and vehicle exclusions apply. Good for avoiding the pricey counter coverage.
  • Baggage Insurance Plan – Coverage for lost or damaged baggage on eligible common-carrier trips paid with the card: up to $1,250 for carry-on and $500 for checked baggage, in excess of the carrier's coverage. Useful if your bag takes a detour.
  • Purchase Protection – Covers eligible new items against accidental damage, theft, or loss for 90 days; typically up to $1,000 per claim and $50,000 per account per year (benefit levels can vary; check your guide). Helpful on phones and gadgets.
  • Extended Warranty – Adds up to one additional year on U.S. manufacturer warranties of 5 years or less for eligible purchases made with the card. Can save you from buying store warranties.
  • Note on trip protections – This card does not list trip cancellation or trip delay insurance; plan accordingly or buy coverage separately if you need it.
